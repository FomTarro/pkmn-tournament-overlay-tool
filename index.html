Welcome to TourKOAL (Tournament Knowledge and Overlay Assistant for Livestreams)!

The goal of this tool is to easily compile player and team information such that it can be quickly displayed on the fly during Pok√©mon tournament matches, helping you build a professional-quality overlay in OBS for your broadcast with as little manual work as possible.

No longer will you need to scramble through hundreds of image files by hand as players reveal which of their Pok√©mon they brought to the game, nor will you need to manually adjust OBS sources when items are consumed or Pok√©mon faint! You can even automatically track and update player pairings and standings to display on stream!

The tool runs entirely in the browser as well, making it very portable and easy to run on venue stream setups without needing to download or install any additional software.

Getting Started
  1. Make sure that your OBS WebSocket Server is Enabled (accessed via Tools ‚Üí WebSocket Server Settings).
    • For OBS Versions 28.0.0 and above, OBS WebSocket Server is built-in.
    • For older OBS Versions, you can download a plugin.
  2. Connect to OBS.
  3. Choose which OBS Scene your Tournament Overlay is in.
  4. Pick sources in that scene to output to (Pok√©mon icons are Browser sources, everything else is a Text source).
  5. Enter player data in the table at the bottom of the page, or import your tournament roster from a TOM file.
  6. You're all set! Pick your players from the dropdown and easily display information about their team!
    • Optional: You can also display pairings and standings in real-time, either by entering the data manually, or by tracking TOM files.
IMPORTANT!
Your privacy, and the privacy of your players, is extremely important! As such, this tool runs entirely within the user's browser. No data, be it manually entered or imported from TOM reports, is being collected or sent to a remote server.

Please be sure to obtain express permission from your players before featuring them and/or their names on stream!
